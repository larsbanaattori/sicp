# SICP

## Background

The aim of this project is to take my programming and computer science skills to the next level by studying through Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP), a classic textbook by MIT professors Harold Abelson and Gerald J. Sussman ([link](https://mitpress.mit.edu/sites/default/files/sicp/index.html)). This particular book was selected due to the praise it has received within the computer science community (see [here](https://teachyourselfcs.com/#programming), [here](https://www.amazon.com/review/R403HR4VL71K8/) and [here](http://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~bh/sicp.html), for example) and the fact that it has stood the test of time - the first edition was published in 1984 and based on the MIT computer science intro course taught by the authors. Almost 40 years later, computer scientists and programmers, both aspiring and experienced ones, are still studying it.

## Goals

My goals for the project are to achieve the following feats by end of June 2023 (a year from project start):

- Read the book from cover to cover
- Complete all exercises in the book
- ...have fun while at it.

## Process

I'll utilize SICP lectures given by the authors in 1986 as the primary supplementary material to help me understand the content of the book ([link](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8FE88AA54363BC46)). These lectures should cover the vast majority of the book. In case additional "tutoring" is needed, I'll enjoy Brian Harvey's (UC Berkeley) 2011 lectures ([link](https://archive.org/details/ucberkeley-webcast-PL3E89002AA9B9879E?sort=titleSorter)).

I'll aim to cover one subsection (e.g. "1.3 Formulating Abstractions with Higher-Order Procedures") at a time and take the following steps for each subsection:

- Read the relevant subsection in the book
- Watch the relevant lecture video(s)
- Complete the exercises.

I'll start off by using DrRacket IDE as the programming environment of choice.
